# Pattern IV — Content Priority Architecture

*Tiered investment aligned to epistemic demonstrability, accessibility, and cross-system leverage. Principled asymmetry, not authorial inclination.*

Full treatment: [`METHODOLOGY.md` § IV](../../METHODOLOGY.md). This pattern is editorial; no code component implements it.

## Problem It Solves

An integral system claims all domains are real and irreducible — but it cannot invest equally in all simultaneously, and a reader encountering the system for the first time cannot absorb everything at once. Without content priority architecture, the system either distributes effort evenly (mediocrity everywhere, excellence nowhere) or follows authorial preference (depth in favoured topics, hollowness in others, with no principled justification for the asymmetry).

## The Solution in Brief

Content priority is determined by the convergence of three criteria.

- **Epistemic demonstrability** — How can this domain prove itself to a sceptical reader? Empirically verifiable domains (Health) score highest; phenomenologically verifiable domains (Presence) score high; metaphysical domains score lower.
- **Accessibility** — How many readers will naturally arrive here? Universally accessible domains (Health — everyone has a body) score highest.
- **Cross-system leverage** — How much does investment here pay dividends across other domains? Centre-investments score highest by definition; *deepening Presence enriches every other pillar*.

The tier that scores highest on all three criteria receives the deepest investment — the most detailed protocols, the most rigorous sourcing, the most layered writing.

## The Alchemical Sequence

The five cartographies of the soul (Indian, Chinese, Shamanic, Greek, Abrahamic) independently encode the same developmental sequence: *prepare the vessel, then fill it with light*. Body before spirit, not because body is superior, but because an unprepared vessel cannot hold what the inward turn delivers. This sequence governs content development as well as individual practice: foundation-tier content deepens first, structural-tier next, flowering-tier last. The system grows the way a tree grows — roots before crown, trunk before canopy.

## The Harmonism Case

- **Tier 1 — Deep Investment.** Health (empirically demonstrable, universally accessible) and Presence (highest cross-system leverage, deepest interior).
- **Tier 2 — Structural Solidity.** Matter, Service — solid, principled, practically useful; less voluminous than Tier 1.
- **Tier 3 — The Crucible.** Relationships — sharp, honest, unflinching; fewer articles but each cuts deep.
- **Tier 4 — The Flowering.** Learning, Nature, Recreation — rich but not voluminous; grows organically with the system.

The asymmetry is principled — it follows from the system's own architecture, not authorial preference. A different tradition will produce a different tier order from the same three-criteria framework.

## Adoption Path

1. For each peripheral pillar of your wheel, score it against the three criteria.
2. Cluster the pillars into 3–4 tiers based on the composite score.
3. Allocate writing, translation, and AI-companion training investment accordingly.
4. Defend the asymmetry to internal collaborators using the three criteria, not preference.
5. Revisit the tiers as the system matures — a Tier 4 pillar that develops a strong empirical demonstration may move up.

## Status

Doctrine-grade. The pattern is an editorial discipline, not a schema. The three criteria are transferable; the resulting tier order is tradition-specific.
