# Pattern II — Centre-Spoke Topology

*Mode of engagement as centre, not domain. Deepening the centre enriches every spoke.*

Full treatment: [`METHODOLOGY.md` § II](../../METHODOLOGY.md). This pattern is architectural; no code component implements it.

## Problem It Solves

Every integral system must answer a political question: what goes at the centre? The answer determines content priority, pedagogical sequence, the system's implicit claim about what matters most. Place body at centre → materialism. Place spirit at centre → escapism. Place community at centre → collectivism. Place individual at centre → libertarianism. Every domain-as-centre choice privileges one and subordinates the others.

## The Solution in Brief

Place at the centre not a *domain* but a *mode of engagement* — the quality of consciousness that makes all domains come alive. In Harmonism this is *Presence*: not a topic (like Health or Learning) but the awareness with which any topic is engaged. The centre is the axis that runs through all spokes, not one spoke among others. The wheel's hub is not competing for territory with the spokes.

## The Architectural Consequence

*Deepening the centre automatically enriches every spoke.* A practitioner cultivating the centre does not thereby neglect any peripheral pillar — they bring greater awareness to all. The centre becomes the highest-leverage investment in the entire system because its returns compound across every domain. Content priority architecture (Pattern IV) follows directly from this insight.

## What Each Tradition's Centre Might Be

- **Contemplative traditions** — the mode of consciousness from which practice flows (Presence, *vipassanā* awareness, *fikr*, *theoria*, *unio mystica*).
- **Traditional medicine** — diagnostic awareness, the practitioner's quality of seeing the patient.
- **Indigenous wisdom** — relational reciprocity, *ayni*-shaped engagement with land and kin.
- **Integral pedagogy** — reflective practice, the metacognitive register from which all learning self-corrects.
- **Civilizational architecture** — Dharma at centre (Harmonism's [Architecture of Harmony](https://harmonism.io/architecture-of-harmony)).

The pattern is to identify what, when deepened, simultaneously enriches every other domain — and place that at centre.

## Adoption Path

1. Survey the candidate centres for your tradition's wheel.
2. Apply the *deepening-test*: does cultivating this principle enrich every peripheral pillar? If yes, candidate centre. If it only enriches some, it is a domain, not a centre.
3. Test against the *political-symmetry test*: does placing this at centre import a doctrinal commitment your tradition would not endorse? If yes, find a more fundamental principle.
4. The correct centre often surprises — it is rarely the most spoken-about domain in the tradition. It is the *quality* of engagement that the most spoken-about domains depend on.

## Status

Doctrine-grade. The pattern is an architectural decision, not a schema. Adoption is editorial — name your centre, defend the choice against the two tests, and let the rest of the architecture cascade from it.
